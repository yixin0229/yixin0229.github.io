01 — Background

Understanding the problem

College students living in dormitories often face challenges transitioning to independent living while sharing space with a new roommate. Differences in personal preferences, if left unaddressed, can quickly escalate into conflicts.

For students, this not only leads to missed opportunities to develop essential interpersonal skills like empathy and conflict resolution but also impacts the college. Unresolved disputes can result in frequent room changes, reassignment fees, logistical burdens, and potential loss of board revenue when students opt for alternative housing.

Therefore, trained Resident Assistants (RAs) play a crucial role in mediating roommate conflicts, fostering a supportive living environment, and reducing administrative costs for the college.

The Human Impact
Students miss opportunities to develop empathy and conflict resolution skills when roommate conflicts go unresolved.
The Institutional Impact
Unresolved disputes lead to frequent room changes, reassignment fees, logistical burdens, and potential loss of board revenue.
The Opportunity
Trained RAs can mediate conflicts, foster supportive living environments, and reduce administrative costs for the college.
02 — Solution

A scenario-based microlearning program

I researched various college resources on conflict resolution strategies to identify key areas where new RAs often struggle and designed the scenario-based microlearning program, which allows RAs to practice essential conflict resolution skills in a simulated environment.

By engaging in realistic scenarios, RAs can refine their decision-making abilities, enabling them to handle real-life situations more effectively and contribute to a stronger dormitory community.

03 — My Process

Following the ADDIE model

To develop this project, I followed the ADDIE model, beginning with the analysis phase. During this phase, I researched the topic to identify the most important aspects of the strategies in managing interpersonal conflicts in the context of dormitory that RAs need to understand and practice. This information guided the creation of a text-based storyboard that outlined the flow of the microlearning program.

Once the storyboard was done, I focused on visual design, creating high-fidelity mockups to visualize the user interface and interactions. Then I moved on to the development phase, where I created the microlearning program using Rise 360 and Vyond to test functionality and gather feedback from peers and professionals.

04 — Action Mapping

From research to learning objectives

While browsing webpages on residence life, several key areas emerged as priorities: identifying sources and signs of conflict, understanding the strategies for effective conflict management, and techniques for building positive relationships.

These insights shaped the learning objectives: at the end of the course, RAs will be able to understand and recognize potential sources and signs of conflict, apply various strategies to manage conflicts between roommates in the dormitory, and assist in building positive relationships between roommates.

05 — Storyboard

Scripting realistic scenarios

Once I finalized the high-priority actions, I designed relatable, scenario-based examples for learners to complete. I wrote scripts in a narrative voice that closely mimics day-to-day dormitory interactions.

For each scenario, learners answer a multiple-choice question in a risk-free environment, allowing them to make mistakes and connect their actions to real-world outcomes. This approach enhances learning and personalizes the experience.

Additionally, at the end of each section, learners are encouraged to reflect by answering open-ended questions, which they can save for further review after class.

Text-based storyboard — narrative voice mimicking dormitory interactions

06 — Full Development

Building the final course

After finalizing the prototype, I moved forward with the full development of the micro-learning course. The course includes informative texts to explain the four strategies and videos that demonstrate their practical applications. The scenes and videos were created in Vyond and integrated into Rise 360.
